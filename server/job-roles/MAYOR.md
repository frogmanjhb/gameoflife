# Mayor - Job Role Document

## Authority Model

- **The Mayor leads decision-making**
- **Council input is required for major decisions**
- **The teacher holds veto power**
- **Powers can be restricted during crises or misconduct**

## Core Purpose

The Mayor is the public leader and co-ordinator of the town. Their role is to balance people, money and land while representing the town's values.

## Tangible Responsibilities

### Daily (Light Touch – 5 minutes)

- Check Town Hub alerts and messages
- Respond to issues raised by:
  - HR Director
  - Police Lieutenant
  - Town Planner
- Clarify rules or decisions when conflicts arise

### Weekly (10–15 minutes total)

- Chair one council meeting
- Approve or reject:
  - land tenders (with Planner)
  - major spending (with Finance)
- Set or adjust:
  - tax rate
  - town priorities
- Deliver a short public announcement (spoken or written)

## Money & Power

- **Sets tax rate**
- **Approves major spending**
- **Cannot move money directly**
- All financial actions go through Finance roles

## Accountability & Failure

Mayor can:
- Be voted out mid-term
- Lose powers temporarily
- Be fined or removed for poor leadership
- Teacher acts as constitutional authority

## Town Hub Permissions

- Post official announcements
- View all town statistics
- Approve council decisions
- Trigger events with teacher approval
- See summaries from all departments

## Physical Board Role

Final sign-off on:
- public buildings
- town-wide layout changes

Visible leader during:
- elections
- disasters
- town meetings

## Job Details

- **Salary**: $10,000 per period
- **Company**: Town Government
- **Location**: Town Hall
- **Category**: Governance & Administration
