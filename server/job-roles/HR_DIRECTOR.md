# HR Director - Job Role Document

## Authority Model

- **Advisory only**
- **Cannot move, remove or assign jobs**
- **Influence comes through evidence, observation and recommendation**
- **Teacher has final authority**

## Core Purpose

The HR Director looks after the wellbeing, participation and fairness of the town's people. This role exists to prevent problems escalating and to ensure everyone is supported to succeed.

## Tangible Responsibilities

### Daily (Active Role – short check-ins)

- Check in with students about:
  - workload
  - role clarity
  - group dynamics
- Observe participation and engagement
- Note concerns, conflicts or strengths
- Encourage struggling students early

### Weekly (15–20 minutes)

- Mediate disputes before they become disciplinary
- Review job performance across the town
- Recommend:
  - bonuses
  - support strategies
  - role adjustments (recommendation only)
- Meet with:
  - Mayor (town morale overview)
  - Teacher (confidential support matters)

## Discipline vs Support

- **Support-first**
- **Discipline is never applied directly**
- **HR focuses on:**
  - listening
  - problem-solving
  - restoring relationships
- **Escalates only when necessary**

## Relationship Structure

- Acts as advisor to:
  - Mayor (town wellbeing)
  - Teacher (learning and behaviour support)
- Does not report publicly
- Operates mostly behind the scenes

## Privacy & Trust

- **HR notes are private**
- **Visible only to:**
  - HR Director
  - Teacher
- **Mayor receives summaries, not details**

## Town Hub Permissions

- View all job assignments
- Submit confidential notes
- Log commendations and concerns
- Flag students needing support (teacher-only)

## Physical Board Role

- **No building authority**
- Present during:
  - mediations
  - council meetings (by invitation)
- Acts as calm facilitator

## Job Details

- **Salary**: $7,200 per period
- **Company**: Town Government
- **Location**: HR Office
- **Category**: Governance & Administration
