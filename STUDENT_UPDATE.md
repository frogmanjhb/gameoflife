# CivicLab — What's New for Students

*A simple guide to recent job and town updates. Last updated: May 2026.*

---

## Quick summary

Jobs in CivicLab are bigger and more connected than before. You can **apply for work**, **level up**, **earn more money**, and **do real town tasks** through your job page. Many roles now have **mini-games**, **new responsibilities**, and **links to other parts of town** (health, police, insurance, lessons, and more).

---

## The Employment Board

Open **Jobs** from the Town Hub to see every role in your town.

Jobs are grouped into sections:

| Section | Examples |
|--------|----------|
| 🏛 Government & Finance | Mayor, Financial Manager, Accountant, HR, Police, Lawyer, Town Planner, Insurance Manager |
| 🏗 Infrastructure & Design | Civil Engineer, Electrical Engineer, Architect |
| 🎓 Education | Principal, Teacher |
| 🏥 Health | Doctor, Nurse |
| 🛍 Economy & Events | Retail Manager, Event Planner, **Entrepreneur** |
| 🎨 Media & Tech | Marketing, Graphic Designer, Journalist, Software Engineer |

### New and updated job names

Most jobs now start with **Assistant** or **Junior** in the title (for example, *Junior Police Lieutenant* instead of *Police Lieutenant*). This is your **entry-level** title.

As you level up, your title changes:

- **Levels 1–3** — Assistant / Junior  
- **Levels 4–8** — Associate  
- **Levels 9–10** — Senior  

The **Mayor** is elected and does not use this title system.

## Applying for a job

1. Open a job card on the Employment Board.  
2. Read the description and requirements.  
3. Fill in the application form.  
4. Answer **yes** to the CV question only if you have **already submitted your CV on Google Classroom**.  
5. Wait for your teacher to approve or deny your application.

**New:** You can **withdraw** a pending application if you change your mind — then apply for a different job later.

**Remember:** You can only hold **one job** at a time, and there is a limit on how many applications you can have open at once.

## Salaries, levels & XP

### Starting pay

Every job starts at **R2,000 per pay period** when you are first assigned (**Level 1**).

### Level up, earn more

| Level | Approx. salary |
|------|----------------|
| 1 | R2,000 |
| 2 | ~R3,444 |
| 3 | ~R4,889 |
| 4–9 | Goes up each level |
| 10 | **R15,000** (maximum) |

### Contractual jobs

Some jobs are marked **CONTRACTUAL**. They pay **1.5× more** at the same level.

Example: Level 10 contractual = **R22,500** (R15,000 × 1.5).

### Experience points (XP)

- You earn **XP** by doing well in your **job challenge game** and other job tasks.  
- XP pushes you toward the **next level**.  
- Check your progress on **My Job** (your job details page).

**Tip:** Do your job tasks regularly. Better performance = faster levels = more money.

---

## Tax — what gets taken from your salary

When your teacher pays salaries, **tax** is taken out based on how much you earn.

**New:** Open **Town Information** and tap **Learn about tax** to see a breakdown for **your current job level** — including estimated tax and take-home pay.

Higher job levels generally mean **higher tax rates** (because you earn more).

## Job challenge games

Almost every job has a **challenge game** on your **My Job** page.

- Play the game to practise your role and earn **money + XP**.  
- There is usually a **daily limit** (often 3 games per day — your teacher can change this).  
- You must play for a **minimum time** before you can submit — rushing through will not count.  
- Games use **real elapsed time**, so keep the tab open while you work.

### All jobs with challenge games

Architect · Accountant · Software Engineer · Marketing Manager · Graphic Designer · Journalist · Event Planner · Financial Manager · HR Director · Police Lieutenant · Lawyer · Town Planner · Civil Engineer · Electrical Engineer · Principal · Teacher · Nurse · Doctor · Retail Manager · Entrepreneur

## Role highlights — what each job can do now

### 🚀 Entrepreneur — Town Business Founder

- Play the **Business Builder** challenge game.  
- Submit **business proposals** for teacher approval.  
- If approved, follow the instructions on your job page to run your town business.  
- Post to the **Town News Board** (headline, story, optional photo) — same as Journalist and Graphic Designer; teacher approves before it goes live. Optional **widgets**: story badge, headline style, colour bar, emoji strip (up to 5).

### 👮 Junior Police Lieutenant

- Log **fines** and **bonuses** for classmates (with evidence).  
- Fines go to the **Lawyer** for review, then to the **teacher** for a final decision.  
- Play the **Police** challenge game to earn XP and practice fine calculations.

### ⚖️ Junior Lawyer

- Review fines logged by Police.  
- **Approve** or **dispute** fines using written evidence only.  
- Disputed fines go to the teacher for a final decision.

### 📊 Junior Chartered Accountant

- Some classmates are **assigned to you** for financial oversight.  
- **Click a client’s name** on My Job to see their balance, transaction history, and recent activity (read-only).  
- Submit **advice and suggestions** for each client — each submission earns **10 XP** and **R500** (from the town treasury).  
- Review and **approve certain transfers** that need accountant sign-off (1 XP per approval).  
- Play the **Accountant** challenge game.

### 🩺 Junior Doctor & 💉 Assistant Nurse

- **Doctor:** Randomly assign **illnesses** to classmates (daily town limit applies). Approve cures when patients pay.  
- **Doctor & Nurse:** Mark the **attendance register** each day.  
- If you get sick, a **screen overlay** appears — wait for the clinic to open, then pay for a cure (or use **health insurance** if you have it).

### 🛡️ Assistant Risk & Insurance Manager

- Review **insurance requests** and **claims** from classmates.  
- Approve or deny health and cyber-insurance claims.  
- Works with the Doctor when insured students need clinic treatment.

### 📚 Assistant Teacher & 🎓 Assistant Principal

- **Assistant Teacher:** Suggest **Five Minute Lessons** for the class to vote on.  
- **Assistant Principal:** Help manage town education tasks on your job page.

### 💻 Assistant Software Engineer

- Build and approve **software tools** for the town.  
- Handle **cyber-attack** assignments when they affect the town.

### 🏗 Engineers & 📐 Architect

- Civil and Electrical Engineers can be involved in **land and building approvals**.  
- All design jobs have challenge games tied to town projects.

### 👥 Assistant HR Director · 💰 Assistant Financial Manager

- Help review **sick notes** submitted by absent students.  
- Play your challenge game and support town HR/finance tasks.

## Other town features linked to jobs

| Feature | What students do |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Five Minute Lessons** | Vote on lesson ideas. Teachers and Assistant Teachers can suggest new ones. |
| **Code Board** | See town codes and announcements posted by certain roles. |
| **Town News** | Read (and for some jobs, post) news with optional badges, styles, colour bars, and emojis. |
| **Insurance** | Apply for health insurance; claims may need your Insurance Manager's approval. |
| **Attendance & sick notes** | If absent, submit a sick note. HR, Financial Manager, or Lawyer may review it. |

## If you do not have a job yet

- Browse the **Employment Board**.  
- Apply for roles that match your interests.  
- Make sure your **CV is on Google Classroom** before you apply.  
- You can still use most of the Town Hub, but some features (like loans) need you to have a job first.

Teachers may also pay a **basic salary** to unemployed students from the town treasury — ask your teacher how this works in your class.

---

## Need help?

- **Job questions** → ask your teacher or the student in that role.  
- **Fines or disputes** → talk to Police, then Lawyer if needed; the teacher makes the final call.  
- **Being sick in-game** → visit the clinic when it opens, or ask your Doctor.  
- **Tax or salary questions** → check **Town Information → Learn about tax**, or ask your Financial Manager.
